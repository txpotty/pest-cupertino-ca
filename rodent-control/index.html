One of the most obvious signs of a rodent infestation is the presence of droppings. Mouse droppings are small and pellet-like, while rat droppings are larger and often spindle-shaped. You'll typically find these near food sources, in pantries, under sinks, along baseboards, and in undisturbed areas like attics and basements. The number and freshness of droppings can give an indication of the size and activity level of the infestation. Old, dry droppings suggest a past problem, while fresh, moist ones indicate an active presence. Handling droppings should be done with caution due to the risk of disease transmission.
